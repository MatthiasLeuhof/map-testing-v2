Food & Finesse

Get to the next stop

Stop 1: Ten Katemarkt

Monday - Saturday, 9am - 5pm

Located a short walk from the Jordaan neighbourhood in the Oud-West area of Amsterdam, Ten Katemarkt offers produce from just over 50 stalls, selling everything from fresh fruits, vegetables and fish. The daily market is a great place to buy your groceries and grab lunch on Saturday. Check out the fry shop. You can get cheap vegetables as well as good bread, great cheese and more exotic fruit. If this is not a great selection, then we don’t know what is!

Stop 2: De Foodhallen

Sunday - Thursday, 11am - 11.30pm

Friday - Saturday, 11am - 1.00am

If you want more exotic food, visit De Foodhallen next door. It is connected to the Ten Katemarkt and used to be an old tram depot. The indoor market is a place where you can experience a whole load of new food concepts from around the globe. Head there for lunch, dinner or a satisfying snack in between. Dishes include sushi hand rolls, Spanish jamon, New Zealand pies, Turkish meze, seafood and an excellent selection of burgers. The food spots change from time to time, so it is worth multiple visits.

Stop 3: Albert Cuyp Markt

Monday - Saturday, 9am - 5pm

Perhaps the most famous market in Amsterdam is the Albert Cuypmarkt, situated in Amsterdam’s De Pijp neighbourhood. It is home to pretty much any Dutch snack your appetite desires. Fresh stroopwafels, pancakes, fries, herring and Dutch cheese are a sample of what is available at the market. The atmosphere is fun and festive, with many tourists visiting to buy souvenirs and get a taste of local Dutch produce. In addition to Dutch street food, you can find many stalls selling cheap clothes, bike accessories and other household additions.

Stop 4: Dappermarkt

Monday - Saturday, 9am - 5pm

Dappermarkt is for those wanting to experience something a bit more local and off the beaten track. The lively market is located in Amsterdam Oost. You will find a mix of all nationalities and backgrounds here. This diverse neighbourhood is the most authentic and usually not too crowded with tourists.

Stop 5: Marqt

Marqt is a sustainable supermarket. Not all products are necessarily organic, but marqt is selling products coming from local suppliers, produced sustainably without artificial supplements. Because of that, it is not super cheap to go grocery shopping here. But we still advise you to come here as a grocery-shopping-experience. There is a lot to see and do in the store: fresh made pasta or creating your own pizza.

Stop 6: STACH

At STACH you can buy fresh organic bread, nice products to fill your daily shopping, fun gifts and more. Or you can just enjoy a good cup of coffee, while working on your computer. There are a lot of affiliates from STACH in Amsterdam, that are all situated on the corner of the streets. The purpose of STACH is to serve the busy city people. After a busy and stressful work day, STACH offers fresh and healthy ready meals of high quality and at a reasonable price. Partly for that reason, the shop is open 7 days a week and until 10pm (and on Sunday until 9 pm).

Stop 7: Toko Dun Yong

Stormsteeg 9, Amsterdam, North Holland

Toko Dun Yong is a huge Asian grocery shop, with fresh vegetables, spices, frozen goods, drinks, kitchenware and decoration. Upstairs in the supermarket is a hidden ramen shop. It is heaven! You can slurp tasty broth while enjoying the beautiful view over the oldest part of Amsterdam. They have a huge supply of Asian grocery products, both fresh and packaged. Plus, they have food workshops in English upstairs.
