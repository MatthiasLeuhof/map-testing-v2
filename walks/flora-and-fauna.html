Flora & Fauna

Stop 1: Klein Dantzig

Klein Dantzig is a beautiful park with different and unique allotments in Amsterdam East. It exists since the beginning of the last century. The approximately 120 gardens are very different of each other. There is a great wealth of trees, flowers and plants. Birds are also very fond of Klein Dantzig. Interested in beekeeping? Then this is the place to go. You can find this spot in the tranquil green oasis called Frakendael Park.

Stop 2: ‘t Nieuwe Diep

‘t Nieuwe Diep is tucked away in a leafy green corner of Flevopark. You find this distillery and tasting room in a polder pump house dating from 1880. The giant house is surrounded by water, fruit trees and herb gardens. It is hidden in the middle of the park; you can enjoy a few beers or a home-made jenever with some snacks and a peaceful view.

Stop 3: Oosterpark

The Oosterpark is an open green space located in Amsterdam Oost. This large city park is filled with ponds and streams, a wading pool for children and plenty of walking paths. Considered Amsterdam’s most multicultural park, it draws a diverse local crowd and hosts a variety of festivals throughout the summer.

Stop 4: Tropenmuseum

Amsterdam's Tropenmuseum is a museum about people from all over the world, housed in an impressive listed building overlooking Amsterdam’s Oosterpark.

Stop 5: Artis

In Artis you can spot funky animals like pink flamingos or striped zebras. But Artis is also home to the biggest Planetarium in the Netherlands. Beyond the zoo's walls, the Plantage district is one of the leafiest and pretty neighbourhoods in all of Amsterdam. It’s a place where nature and cultural heritage come together. Come experience it yourself!

Stop 6: Hortus Botanicus

Amsterdam’s Hortus Botanicus contains over 6,000 different indigenous and non-native trees and plants. In addition to outdoor gardens, they have a café and a ‘hothouse’ that emulates three tropical environments. Hundreds of majestic butterflies can be found inside the Botanicus’ Butterfly Greenhouse. Entry is €9 for an adult, or €5 with a student card.

Stop 7: De Hoftuin

De Hoftuin is a quiet spot hidden behind the Hermitage museum. This secret garden café is the place to go for a high tea, lunch or a drink. The restaurant uses its own herbs and veggies for the dishes. After your meal, you can take a look at the flowers and sculptures in the gardens. Entry is free.

Stop 8: Nemo

Did you know that museum NEMO has a green roof? Here you can enjoy beachy vibes and a magnificent view. It is hard to miss this giant green structure rising like the hull of a cruise liner from the Oosterdok. The building houses an interactive science museum. If you are looking for somewhere to bask in the sun, then head straight to the building’s top-deck. The access to the top-deck is free and you can take your own picnic.
